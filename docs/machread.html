MACHINE READABLE (2025)

To be machine readable, data or text must be in a form that a computer can process. Increasingly machine readability is being privileged over legibility as pathways and processes are streamlined and managed digitally. Machine readable codes and images are integral for digital infrastructures that assist systems of power which dictate material circumstances for the working class.

Machine Readable is a durational work that considers these dynamics between readability and legibility, touching upon issues of algorthmic centralisation and erasure.
